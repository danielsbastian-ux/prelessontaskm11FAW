4.1 — From Example to Analysis

Backing up a claim takes two steps: Evidence (a specific example, statistic, or observation) and Analysis (a sentence explaining why that evidence supports the claim). Use this to answer Part A and Part B below.

Part A — Evidence or Analysis? (1 point each)

Question 1
"A recent city report found that only 12% of residents use public transit for daily commutes."
Question 2
"This low figure suggests that convenience, not cost, is the main barrier to adoption, since transit fares remain cheaper than fuel."
Question 3
"For instance, one factory reduced water waste by 40% after installing sensor-based taps."

Part B — Expand the Example (4 points)

Question 4
"Many small businesses closed during the pandemic. For example, several family-owned restaurants in the area shut down permanently."
Aim for one well-developed sentence that answers "so what?"
